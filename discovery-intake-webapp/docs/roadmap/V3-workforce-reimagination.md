# V3 workforce-reimagination — near-future capstone (spec of record)

> **Status:** documented **near-future capstone — NOT current scope.** Earned only after the
> structural axis (V3-15 → V3-19) ships. Spec of record in the V3-x kickoff style. Posture when
> eventually built: **push-only + plan-then-stop**, gate strictly above the standing floor, 0
> fail, no live model. Inherits every binding guardrail in `CLAUDE.md`.

## Premise

**Future-state role and workforce design, built ON TOP of the structural axis.** Where the
structural axis (`docs/roadmap/V3-structural-axis.md`) describes the work *as it is today* —
typed steps, handoffs, friction, role profiles — this capstone supports reasoning about how the
work *could be redesigned*: which role profiles change, where capacity is created, which friction
is removed.

## Hard dependency

**Requires V3-15 through V3-19.** This is exactly why it is **near-future, not current scope** —
it has nothing defensible to reason over until the structural axis exists. Typed steps (V3-15),
first-class handoffs/decisions (V3-16), user-sourced friction (V3-17), the role-centric pivot
(V3-18), and the department heatmap (V3-19) are its inputs. It is not plannable before those land.

## Division of labor (binding)

- **The tool generates the defensible INPUTS:** typed steps, handoffs, friction, role profiles,
  and capacity (from V3-14). These are evidence-backed, provenance-carrying, estimate-labelled.
- **Humans do the judgment-heavy reimagining.** The redesign of roles and the org is human work.
  The tool does not propose a target org, a headcount, or an automation verdict — it equips the
  people who do that work with honest inputs.

## Method

**Explicit scenario modeling with stated assumptions + provenance** — the **same discipline as
the business-case engine**: mark estimates as estimates, keep computed separate from inferred,
show every assumption, never blend speculative figures with measured ones. A future workforce is
**inherently speculative**; this discipline is precisely what makes a speculative analysis
**defensible** rather than a guess with a chart on it. Each scenario is snapshot-backed and
recomputed only on explicit user action (the trust model, unchanged).

## Framing (binding)

**Capacity created · leverage gained · friction removed — NEVER headcount reduction.** This is a
hard, model-and-label-level rule (inherited from the V3-structural-axis caution): the capstone
does not compute, store, or label a headcount-cut or "% automatable" figure. Its **audience is
the transformation / org-design office** — a different audience than the per-engagement consultant
— and the framing must stay leverage-and-capacity oriented for that audience to trust the inputs.
